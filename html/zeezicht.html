Zeezicht

With UX research and unity, I created an augmented reality game to let kids learn about Sea pollution.

My project, ZeeZicht, focuses on designing an interactive, child-friendly Augmented Reality (AR) experience that utilizes gamification. The goal is to spark curiosity in children visiting aquariums while raising awareness about plastic pollution. Through a treasure hunt and mini-games, Rico, a virtual guide, leads children on an educational journey through the underwater world.

The project began with the question: ‘How can I design an interactive, child-friendly augmented reality experience using gamification that stimulates children's curiosity about aquariums?’ As the process unfolded, I collaborated with Blijdorp Zoo and tailored the app specifically for their needs. During this collaboration, I realized that educating children about marine pollution was a significant focus for Blijdorp. This insight shaped my design, and I began integrating information about plastic pollution into the gameplay.

To ensure ZeeZicht would both engage and educate, I conducted several interviews with educators and children. These interviews informed the design of the AR game, which combines digital interaction with real-life observation activities. Rico, the virtual guide, explains the dangers of plastic pollution and encourages children to complete fun, interactive tasks both in the app and in the aquarium environment.

The game includes gamification elements like rewards, obstacles, and time limits to keep it engaging and challenging. I developed the prototype using Unity, creating an immersive experience that combines technology with physical exploration. The blend of education, entertainment, and hands-on learning ensures that children stay curious while becoming more aware of environmental issues in a fun and interactive way.
