Book

With UX research, I created a book to discuss difficult topics with your child.

The Openhearted Adolescence Book is a fun and helpful guide designed to improve communication between parents/caregivers and teenagers. It covers topics like relationships, feelings of safety, sadness, vaping/smoking, alcohol, separation, and romantic feelings. The goal of the book is to create a comfortable and open environment where both parties can communicate positively.

Why this design? The concept of the Openhearted Adolescence Book emerged from the need to bridge the communication gap between parents/caregivers and teenagers, especially in times when communication can be challenging. Choosing a book format is based on the positive responses from kids and experts, seeing the book as an approachable and creative way to discuss sensitive topics. The book aims to help parents and teenagers understand each other better through positive and open conversations. We collaborated with experts like Annet Peetoom and Magrethe to ensure the book provides useful tips and ideas to enhance communication. The goal is to ask questions, initiate discussions, and bring parents and teenagers closer through positive and open conversations.

How to use the book:

  • Choose a topic: Both parties jointly select the topic they want to discuss.
  • Open the scratch-off area: Beneath the scratch-off area is a question or statement about the chosen topic.
  • Write your response: Both parties write their thoughts, feelings, or answers to the question or statement.
  • Share with each other: Exchange each other's responses and discuss them in an open and respectful manner.
